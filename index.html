About Tic-Tac-Toe

Tic-tac-toe is traditionally a paper-and-pencil game for two players to take turns marking the spaces in a three-by-three grid with an "X" or "O." The player who succeeds in placing three of their marks in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row is the winner.

Today, many variations of tic-tac-toe exist and it can be played on paper or a computer and against a real or virtual opponent. Even smart TVs have the game available for download and free play.

Tic-tac-toe tournaments can fetch as much as $450,000 in prize money!

Versions of Tic-Tac-Toe

Tic-tac-toe has many variants for hours and hours of fun, including:

  1. Misère Tic-Tac-Toe: Exactly the same as regular tic-tac-toe, but you win by forcing your opponent into getting three-in-a-row. You win by losing!
  2. Gomoku: The board is 15x15 and you must get exactly five-in-a-row to win. Six-in-a-row doesn't count!
  3. Wild Tic-Tac-Toe: Uses a standard board, but both players can play as X and O. First to make a three-in-a-row using either shape wins.
  4. Order and Chaos: The board is a 6×6 grid. Both players can play as X and O. One person (Order) wants to get five-in-a-row of either shape. The other player (Chaos) tries to prevent any five-in-a-rows (so, Chaos wants a cat’s game).
  5. Notakto: Played on three standard boards at once. Both players play as X. You can play on any of the boards on your turn. The first person to complete a three-in-a-row loses.
